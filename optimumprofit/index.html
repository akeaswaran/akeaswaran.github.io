Inventory Optimization

Our Demand-Driven Inventory Planner calculates inventory targets that meet your customer service objectives at the lowest possible inventory cost while considering the impact of both demand and lead time variability. DDIP is based on Lean Manufacturing principles and reacts to changes in demand. DDIP calculates a "Re-Order Point" and "Order Up-To" level for each item that can be entered into a company's Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) or accounting system to create replenishment orders. DDIP can also be used as a "stand-alone" system that only needs the on-hand inventory and on-order purchase quantities entered to calculate the replenishment quantities for each item. DDIP is applicable for single-stage and multi-tier environments. In a multi-tier environment there is some additional cost optimization logic that meets your customer service goals while maximizing the impact of cost difference between tiers.

Supply Chain Network Optimization

Our Strategic Network Optimization (SNO) solver creates a plan to source and distribute material throughout your supply chain network at the highest profit (or lowest cost) possible while respecting all of your real world manufacturing and distribution constraints. SNO uses a mixed-integer linear program to generate the cost optimal solution. SNO is typically used for strategic decision making by running multiple scenarios and comparing the results. The supply chain network can include suppliers, manufacturing plants, distribution centers and customers along with all transportation modes and routes. Typical scenarios may include changes in forecasted demand, the impact of additional manufacturing resources, outsourcing of transportation and analyzing which plants to expand or close. The constraints in the network model can vary from very detailed machine level capacities to high-level plant and transportation capacities.
