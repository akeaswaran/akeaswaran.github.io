DDIP Background & Logic

Our Demand-Driven Inventory Planner calculates inventory targets that meet your customer service objectives at the lowest possible inventory cost while considering the impact of both demand and lead time variability.  DDIP is based on Lean Manufacturing principles and reacts to changes in demand.  DDIP calculates a "Re-Order Point" and "Order Up-To" level for each item that can be entered into a company's Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) or accounting system to create replenishment orders.  Select DDIP Background & Logic for a detailed explanation of this functionality.

DDIP is applicable for single-stage and multi-tier environments.  In a multi-tier environment there is some additional cost optimization logic that meets your customer service goals while considering the impact of cost difference between tiers. 

ERP Integration

The "Re-Order Point" and "Order Up-To" level for each item calculated by DDIP can be imported into a company's Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system to calculate optimal replenishment orders.  In the case study described above the ERP system is MAS 90 from Sage Software (the exact same approach is also used for integration with MAS 200 from Sage Software).  DDIP can also be used as a "stand-alone" system that only needs the on-hand inventory and on-order purchase quantities entered to calculate the replenishment quantities for each item.
